Public Wi-Fi Safety

Public Wi-Fi is convenient… and risky. Here's how to protect yourself.

What’s the Risk?

Anyone connected to an open or poorly secured Wi-Fi network — like those in cafés, trains, or even university lounges — can potentially intercept your data. It is like reading you password out loud in a room. Attackers can use the information obtained from wifi for identity theft, infecting your device with malware, stealing your passwords, gaining remote control of your device, and once you start working and you connect to a vulnerable wifi in cafés, stealing your business information.

Tips to protect yourself

  • Use a VPN: It encrypts your traffic, even on public networks.
  • Stick to HTTPS sites: Look for the lock icon in the address bar.
  • Turn off auto-connect: Disable automatic Wi-Fi joining on your device.
  • Check the names of the Wi-Fi before trusting to connect: Sometimes hackers publish fake wifi networks that look legitimate. Ex. AirportWIFI#?
  • Use antivirus: this will scan malware and virus.
  • Do not share files: Hackers can intercept your communication through wifi network. Do not share files containing information that you do not want others to see.

When in doubt, switch to mobile data or use a VPN.
