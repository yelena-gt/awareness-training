Password Safety

Passwords are critical defenses to protect your accounts, but it can be no use if you don't set them properly.

Check your Password Creation Skill

Before scrolling, test your password skill using the Bitwarden site to see how much time it will take to crack the password you create. But do not use the real passwords you are using.

Common Mistakes Many People Make

  • Using the same password for every site
  • Using short or guessable passwords like "123456", "unsw2025" or using your personal information such as birthdays
  • Storing passwords in notes apps or screenshots
  • Never changing your passwords once you set them
  • Writing down passwords in places where others can see such as stickypads on your desk

Try Creating a Passphrase

A passphrase is a combination of random words with at least 14 characters in length, that uses symbols, numbers, and sometimes even a space to represent letters in a word.

Look around your room, pick four random words you find. Here's an example:

c0mputer-shirt-bo0k-PEN

This is strong, memorable, and easy to type! Subsitute some letters with symbols such as @ and 0 to make it more complex!

Now, test your passphrase using the Bitwarden site to see how much time it will take to crack it.

Use Password Managers

Memorizing all the unique passwords may sound almost impossible. In that case, use a secure password manager.

  • Bitwarden (Free)
  • PassWord App (on your Iphone)

These tools can store your passwords, generate new ones, and autofill them securely.
